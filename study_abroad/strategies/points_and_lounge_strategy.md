# Points and Lounge Strategy: The World of Hyatt & Bilt Rewards Playbook

This consolidated guide details the strategy for maximizing World of Hyatt (WoH) points—transferred primarily from Bilt Rewards—for aspirational travel and high-value redemptions during a study abroad semester.

---

## 1. High-Value Redemption Strategy (Hyatt Transfers)

The optimal strategy for Bilt Rewards is a 1:1 transfer to World of Hyatt, which often yields a redemption value of 2+ cents per point (CPP), nearly doubling the value compared to the Bilt Travel Portal (1.25 CPP).

### A. Experience Splurge Redemptions (Cat 4-8)

## 3. The Bilt "Home Away From Home" (HAFH) & Credit Burn Strategy

The Bilt HAFH portal allows booking non-Hyatt luxury hotels (like Four Seasons, Aman, etc.) using points at a fixed **1.25 CPP**, but the key benefit is the inclusion of VIP perks (free breakfast, $100 credit, upgrade). This is best used for a one-off "treat" or to burn Bilt travel credits.

### A. Copenhagen (Lund Hub) Specific Strategy

The ideal local target for a luxury treat that maximizes the HAFH benefits is in Copenhagen.

| Property | Price Estimate (Nightly Low) | HAFH Value Proposition |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Hotel Sanders** | **From ~$324 USD** | **PERFECT Credit Burn.** Falls directly in the sweet spot ($250-$450 USD). Maximize the $100 property credit and complimentary breakfast for a high-end experience at a manageable cost. |
| **Villa Copenhagen** | Varies, high-end | Likely above $400. Still a great HAFH option, but Sanders offers better points value. |
