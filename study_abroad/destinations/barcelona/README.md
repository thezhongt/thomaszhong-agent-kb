## 1. Universitat Pompeu Fabra (UPF) Deep Dive

# Universitat Pompeu Fabra (UPF), Barcelona - Study Abroad Deep Dive

This deep dive focuses on the key logistical and academic factors for a CS/Tech student considering a study abroad semester at UPF in Barcelona.

---

## 1. Campus Location (CS/Engineering)

| Status | Location | Vibe |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Confirmed** | **Poblenou Campus** (Roc Boronat, 138) | Modern, Innovative (22@ District) |

The Computer Science (CS) and Engineering programs are primarily housed at the **Poblenou Campus**, often referred to as the Communication Campus. This location is excellent as it is situated in the **22@ District**, Barcelona's innovation and tech hub, putting the student right in a center of start-ups and modern industrial design.

## 2. Airport Logistics (Poblenou Campus to BCN T1)

The Poblenou Campus is well-connected, but getting to Barcelona El Prat (BCN) Terminal 1 (T1, the Vueling hub) requires one transfer.

*   **Closest Metro:** Llacuna (L4 line), near the Roc Boronat campus.
*   **Best Route:** Take the **Metro L4** from Llacuna and transfer to the **L9 Sud** (the airport line) at a key hub like La Pau or Verdaguer (or a similar central stop). The L9 Sud connects directly to BCN T1 and T2.
*   **Direct Line:** There is **no direct train/metro/bus** line from the Poblenou area to BCN T1.
*   **Total Travel Time:** Expect the journey to take **45 to 60 minutes** via public transport, including the transfer and walking to the station. A taxi/rideshare is 20-30 minutes, depending on traffic.

## 3. Neighborhood Vibe (Poblenou)

Poblenou is highly recommended for a student and an excellent location for UPF.

*   **Vibe:** It is described as a "gentrifying, hipster hotspot." It has transformed from an industrial area into the modern 22@ tech/innovation district. It offers a relaxed, village-like atmosphere with a thriving cultural and creative scene, including popular open-air markets.
*   **Walkability:** Extremely walkable, with wide streets and a good mix of residential calm and commercial activity.
*   **Student-Friendly:** Yes. Proximity to the UPF campus and comparatively lower rent (versus Ciutat Vella) makes it popular with students.
*   **Beach Proximity:** Very high. The campus and most of the neighborhood are a short **10 to 20-minute walk** from the popular beaches of Bogatell and Mar Bella.

## 4. Academic Calendar

This is a **major benefit** for a student with a January internship.

*   **System:** UPF operates on a **Trimester System**, not a traditional semester.
*   **Fall Trimester (T1):** The first trimester runs from **September to mid-December**.
*   **Exams:** The exam period for Trimester 1 also concludes in December.
*   **Conclusion:** Unlike many Spanish universities that hold final exams in January, UPF's Trimester system allows students to finish all academic obligations before the winter holidays, making it **ideal for a student who needs to start a January internship**.

## 5. Cost of Living (Shared Apartment Room)

Rent for a single room in a shared flat in Poblenou is competitive for a sought-after area near the beach.

*   **Range:** While listings start lower, a realistic budget for a decent, well-located room in a shared student apartment in Poblenou is estimated to be between **€450 and €650 per month**.
*   **Total Monthly Cost:** Students should budget for an additional €200–€300 per month for utilities, transport, and groceries.

---

## Vesper's Verdict: UPF for a "Travel Heavy" Semester

**VERDICT: ✅ WORKS WELL (Favorable)**

UPF in Poblenou is a great fit for a "Travel Heavy" semester, primarily due to two factors:

1.  **The Trimester Calendar:** Finishing classes and exams entirely by **mid-December** provides the student with the full four-week winter break for travel and completely avoids any conflict with a January internship start date.
2.  **Location Logistics:** The campus is in the trendy, well-connected 22@ district, which offers a great local vibe and quick access to the beach. While travel to the airport isn't "direct," the Barcelona Metro is efficient, making weekend travel manageable. The vibrant, less-touristed Poblenou neighborhood itself provides an ideal base.

## 2. Priority Pass Lounge Reality Check

The key challenge for a budget traveler is that LCC flights often depart from the Schengen zone (for travel within the EU), while the highest-rated lounges are sometimes sequestered in the Non-Schengen or long-haul areas. Both Spanish hubs handle this well, offering strong options in their LCC-heavy terminals.

### Madrid-Barajas (MAD)

| Lounge Name | Terminal/Zone | Access Reality for Student | Quality/Reputation |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Sala VIP Puerta de Alcalá** | T2 (Schengen) | **Easy Access.** This lounge is located near the gates for domestic and Schengen flights, meaning LCC trips to LIS, FCO, BER, etc., will be able to access it. | Good reputation, contemporary design, includes showers. |
| **Sala VIP Cibeles** | T1 (Non-Schengen) | **Access if flying Non-Schengen.** Used for LCC flights to destinations like London (STN/LGW) or Marrakech (RAK). | Generally high quality, but requires passing passport control. |

**MAD Reality Check:** Excellent access. The Puerta de Alcalá lounge in T2 covers the vast majority of budget European weekend trips.

### Barcelona-El Prat (BCN)

| Lounge Name | Terminal/Zone | Access Reality for Student | Quality/Reputation |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Sala VIP Pau Casals** | T1 (Schengen) | **The Best Access.** T1 is the Vueling hub. This lounge is Schengen-exclusive and located in the main departure area, perfect for almost all student European weekend trips. | **Excellent reputation.** Spacious, high-end food/drink, often considered one of the best PP lounges in Europe. |
| **Sala VIP Joan Miró** | T1 (Non-Schengen) | **Access if flying Non-Schengen.** Used for LCC flights to destinations like the UK or Ireland. | Very high quality, similar to Pau Casals, making BCN's T1 a very strong option regardless of Schengen status. |

**BCN Reality Check:** Exceptional access. Both major lounges are in the main LCC terminal (T1), offering top-tier quality in both Schengen and Non-Schengen zones.

## 3. The "Winner" Verdict: MAD vs. BCN vs. CPH

For a student traveler in the context of a European study abroad program, the ideal travel hub balances budget flight access with convenience and lounge quality.

| Criterion | Madrid (MAD) | Barcelona (BCN) | Copenhagen (CPH) |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Budget Flight Price** | Good, slightly higher volume hub for Iberia. | **Excellent.** Vueling hub provides best LCC coverage and lowest floor prices. | Fair, but prices are generally much higher due to Scandinavia's higher cost base. |
| **Flight Network** | Strong, excellent long-haul, good European. | **Superior European/Sun network.** | Strong for Northern Europe/Scandinavia, poor for Southern Europe/North Africa. |
| **Lounge Quality/Access** | Very Good (Good Schengen coverage in T2, High-quality Non-Schengen in T1). | **Excellent.** Two high-rated lounges (Pau Casals/Joan Miró) in the main LCC terminal (T1). | Subpar. Priority Pass lounges are generally lower-rated and often crowded. |
| **Cost of Living (Student)** | Lower than BCN. | Higher than MAD. | Significantly Higher. |
| **Winner (Travel Hub Focus)** | Strong Contender. | **Clear Winner.** | Weak Contender. |

### Final Conclusion: Barcelona (BCN)

**Barcelona (BCN) is the clear winner for the student traveler.**

While Madrid offers a slightly lower cost of living, BCN's superior LCC network and marginally lower flight prices will result in significant savings over the course of a semester of weekend trips. Critically, BCN's Priority Pass lounges are concentrated in Terminal 1 (Vueling's hub) and are consistently rated as some of the best airport lounges in Europe, providing a superior experience for both Schengen (Pau Casals) and Non-Schengen (Joan Miró) budget travel.

A student based in CPH would find their travel costs at least 50-100% higher and their lounge experience notably worse. Spain is the better option, and BCN edges out MAD on the combined factors of cost and comfort.
